Matthew Walton intro bio

I'm Matthew Walton, a FullStack Developer from Missouri. I grew up in Mount Vernon, Washington, during the 80s and 90s. My grandpa was a computer enthusiast who inspired me to explore the world of computers. He would always encourage me to spend as much time as possible on the computer. He even told me that I needed to learn as many computer skills as possible, because one day when I became older, everyone would be walking around with computers in their hands.I thought my grandpa was crazy at the time. I didn't believe him until around 2006 or 2007, six or seven years after his death, when I saw the first iPhone commercial. That's when I started to believe him. I've mainly worked with Windows computers because they're convenient and widely available. However, I've never been a fan of Windows; the reason is simple. Windows, or Microsoft in general, makes everything more difficult, and they seem to presure you into their products. I don't like Apple products either. I can't stand Apple products. My reasons for not liking Apple are that in the early 2000s, Apple tried to say that the Apple Mac wasn't a PC, it was a Mac. Yet Steve Jobs called Apple computers in the '70s and '80s a PC, a personal computer. He coined the buzzword "PC," and then later on in the early 2000s went back and said, "Oh, it's not a PC, it's a Mac," as if a Mac were fundamentally different from a PC.

I like Linux, but I've used Windows most of my life. So I used Windows with WSL and Linux Ubuntu in my terminal. I don't like gaming with my computers either. I would rather have a dedicated machine for that, like a PS4 or PS5. I also avoid anti-virus software on my computers. I find it slows them down, it's often unhelpful, and it's usually not lightweight, Beside's windows has build-in security and same with google chrome. I lived through a lot of computer history, and it has envolution along side of me growing up. I remember the days before the internet and the excitement when residential internet arrived, making it accessible to everyone. I remember the AOL days, dial-up connections, and Netscape, my first browser, as well as the advent of email. When our teacher took us to the computer lab to learn about email, I told her I wasn't interested. I didn't see the need for it.She said, "Well, you might need to send an email someday." I replied, "Most people I know don't even know what the internet is, let alone email!" Little did she know, I already knew how to send emails. I was an avid Napster user, sharing massive music files. It was a revolutionary concept at the time. Even Spotify can't compare to the old music-sharing system. Spotify emerged as a response to music piracy, which didn't disappear with Napster. I'm proud to say that I've been a FullStack Developer for over 3 years now. I've worked on a variety of projects. I'm passionate about helping others learn about technology and coding. I'm also a big fan of open-source software. I'm excited to see what the future holds for me as a developer. I'm always learning new things and I'm excited to continue to grow in my career. I just need a job that can give me my indepence to continue, I still do it anyways everyday after work.
